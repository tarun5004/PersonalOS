# Personas

Status: Approved for V1 planning  
Source of truth: Master Prompt V4

## 1. Student

### Profile

A student manages classes, assignments, study sessions, and personal routines. They may use multiple apps today and want a simple dashboard for daily focus.

### Goals

- See what needs to be done today.
- Build consistent study and wellness habits.
- Track weekly progress without creating complex systems.

### Pain Points

- Assignments and habits live in separate tools.
- Progress is hard to see over time.
- Overly complex apps become another chore.

### V1 Needs

- Daily task list with due dates and priorities.
- Habit check-ins with a monthly view.
- Dashboard summary for today's progress.
- Light and dark theme support for different study environments.

## 2. Developer

### Profile

A developer tracks project tasks, learning goals, job preparation, and recurring routines.

### Goals

- Keep coding and learning tasks organized.
- Build habits around practice, reading, exercise, or applications.
- Self-host or customize the tool.

### Pain Points

- Productivity tools often hide implementation details.
- Existing dashboards may not be easy to modify.
- Server-state and auth flows can be hard to reason about in open-source apps.

### V1 Needs

- Clean task and habit workflows.
- Transparent docs and architecture.
- Reusable components and feature folders.
- Self-hostable project direction.

## 3. Job Seeker

### Profile

A job seeker balances applications, interviews, portfolio work, study routines, and follow-ups.

### Goals

- Track application-related tasks.
- Maintain daily preparation habits.
- Review consistency across the week.

### Pain Points

- Progress feels scattered across notes, spreadsheets, and calendars.
- It is easy to miss follow-ups or lose momentum.
- Motivation drops when progress is invisible.

### V1 Needs

- Task statuses for application progress.
- Habit streaks for preparation consistency.
- Weekly analytics for visible momentum.

## 4. Freelancer

### Profile

A freelancer manages client work, admin tasks, personal routines, and deadlines.

### Goals

- Keep daily work visible.
- Prioritize tasks across client and personal responsibilities.
- Track habits that support sustainable work.

### Pain Points

- Workflows change often.
- Too many planning tools become fragmented.
- Dashboards can become noisy.

### V1 Needs

- Focused dashboard.
- Priority-based task management.
- Habit check-ins that do not require complex configuration.

## 5. Remote Worker

### Profile

A remote worker wants structure around daily responsibilities, health routines, and weekly progress.

### Goals

- Start the day with a clear view.
- Maintain routines while working independently.
- Understand whether the week is on track.

### Pain Points

- Remote work can blur work and personal routines.
- Daily progress may be hard to recognize.
- Task tools may not include personal habit visibility.

### V1 Needs

- Today's summary.
- Current streak and habit score.
- Simple analytics without overwhelming detail.
